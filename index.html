Mission Objectives

What we aim to achieve as a band ministry

Objective 01
✕

Accessible Training

Provide accessible and engaging instrument training for people of all ages so they can actively participate and serve in the worship ministry of the church.

Objective 02
△

Purposeful Worship

Help learners grow not only in skill but in understanding the purpose of music in worship — to honor God, not just to play well.

Objective 03
□

Band Unity

Build a cohesive band culture where each instrument understands its role and listens actively to serve the team and the worship leader.

Objective 04
○

Safe & Joyful Space

Create a Christ-centered environment where individuals from different walks of life grow together and experience the joy of making music for God.

Assessment Framework

How we track, measure, and celebrate growth throughout the 3-month program.

Registration Assessment

Collected at sign-up via Google Forms

Registration Form Fields

  • Full Name
  • Age (Optional)
  • Instrument of Choice
  • Skill Level: Beginner / Intermediate / Advanced
  • Available Sundays (June–August 2026)
  • Why do you want to join?

Skill Grouping

  • Beginners: Start from scratch — basics, posture, first notes
  • Intermediate: Fast-tracked to Month 1 Week 3+ content
  • Advanced: Mentor role in group; personal growth goals set with trainer

Monthly Progress Checkpoints

End of each month — trainers assess growth

🎹 Piano Checkpoints

  • Month 1: Identifies all 12 notes; plays C major scale; 3-chord song with both hands
  • Month 2: Plays inversions, sus chord; 2 full worship songs with left hand bass
  • Month 3: Plays in full band context, responds to cues; performance-ready

🎸 Acoustic Checkpoints

  • Month 1: Plays G, C, D, Em, Am with clean transitions; basic strumming patterns
  • Month 2: Plays barre chord F; uses capo; song in 2 different keys
  • Month 3: Plays in full band context; performance-ready

⚡ Electric Checkpoints

  • Month 1: Understands signal chain; plays pentatonic scale & a simple riff
  • Month 2: Plays a lead run; sets up delay sound; ambient fills; knows when to rest
  • Month 3: Plays in band context with dynamic control; performance-ready

🎵 Bass Checkpoints

  • Month 1: Plays root notes over chord progression; basic walking line
  • Month 2: Syncs with kick drum; root-3rd-5th line over full songs
  • Month 3: Locks with drums in full band; performance-ready

🥁 Drums Checkpoints

  • Month 1: Plays basic rock beat; stays in time with metronome; hi-hat variations
  • Month 2: Controls dynamics; plays quietly in worship; 4-bar fills
  • Month 3: Leads band timing in full ensemble; performance-ready

🎼 Joint / Band Checkpoints

  • Month 1: All trainees share the same musical vocabulary
  • Month 2: All trainees know song parts & hand signals; can transpose
  • Month 3: Full band plays 3 songs live for the congregation

Take-Home Self-Assessment

Distributed at the end of each Sunday session

Each handout includes a short self-assessment section: (1) What I learned today, (2) What I still need to practice, (3) My confidence level this week (1–5), (4) One thing I want to bring to God through my playing. Optional to submit back but encouraged for trainer review.

Final Assessment — Month 3

Criteria for completion and commissioning

Completion Criteria

  • Attended at least 70% of all Sunday sessions
  • Can play their instrument in a full worship song
  • Demonstrated understanding of band dynamics and hand signals
  • Participated in at least one full band rehearsal
  • Shows a heart posture of worship, not just performance

Commissioning Output

  • Certificate of Completion issued
  • Added to worship team talent pool roster
  • Assigned a mentor from the existing worship team
  • Given a schedule slot for their first actual Sunday service
  • Invited to join ongoing band ministry discipleship
